## Introduction

Sensors and actuators form the foundation of a robot's interaction with the physical world. Sensors allow robots to perceive their environment, while actuators enable them to act upon it. Together, they create the sensory-motor loop that is essential for all robotic systems, from simple mobile robots to complex humanoid platforms.

The choice and integration of sensors and actuators significantly impact a robot's capabilities, performance, and applications. A robot's "senses" determine what information it can gather from its environment, while its "muscles" determine what actions it can perform. Understanding these components is crucial for designing effective robotic systems.

In robotics, we often speak of the sensorimotor loop: sensors provide input about the environment, the robot processes this information, and actuators produce actions that change the environment. This loop is fundamental to all autonomous systems and forms the basis for more complex behaviors and learning.

This chapter explores the various types of sensors and actuators used in robotics, their operating principles, specifications, and integration into robotic systems. We'll examine both the technical aspects and practical considerations for selecting and implementing these critical components.
