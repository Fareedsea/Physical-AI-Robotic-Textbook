# References

This page contains references and further reading materials for the Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics Textbook.

## Books

1. Siciliano, B., & Khatib, O. (Eds.). (2016). *Springer Handbook of Robotics* (2nd ed.). Springer. - Comprehensive reference covering all aspects of robotics research and applications.

2. Spong, M. W., Hutchinson, S., & Vidyasagar, M. (2006). *Robot Modeling and Control*. John Wiley & Sons. - Classic textbook on robot dynamics and control theory.

3. Craig, J. J. (2005). *Introduction to Robotics: Mechanics and Control* (3rd ed.). Pearson Prentice Hall. - Fundamental text on robotics mechanics and control.

4. Thrun, S., Burgard, W., & Fox, D. (2005). *Probabilistic Robotics*. MIT Press. - Authoritative text on probabilistic approaches to robotics.

5. Goodwin, G. C., Graebe, S. F., & Salgado, M. E. (2005). *Control System Design*. Prentice Hall. - Comprehensive coverage of control system design principles.

6. Gonzalez, R. C., & Woods, R. E. (2018). *Digital Image Processing* (4th ed.). Pearson. - Standard reference for image processing techniques.

7. Khatib, O., Park, H. J., Bjoerkman, M., & Hirose, M. (Eds.). (2019). *Humanoid Robotics: A Reference* (2nd ed.). Springer. - Comprehensive reference on humanoid robotics.

8. Breazeal, C. (2002). *Designing Sociable Robots*. MIT Press. - Pioneering work on social robotics and human-robot interaction.

9. Murphy, R. R. (2019). *Introduction to AI Robotics* (2nd ed.). MIT Press. - Comprehensive introduction to AI techniques in robotics.

10. Latombe, J. C. (2012). *Robot Motion Planning*. Springer Science & Business Media. - Classic text on motion planning algorithms.

## Journal Articles

1. Kober, J., Bagnell, J. A., & Peters, J. (2013). Reinforcement learning in robotics: A survey. *The International Journal of Robotics Research*, 32(11), 1238-1274. - Comprehensive survey of reinforcement learning applications in robotics.

2. Siciliano, B., & Khatib, O. (2016). *Encyclopedia of Robotics*. Springer. - Comprehensive reference with articles on all major robotics topics.

3. Goodrich, M. A., & Schultz, A. C. (2007). Human-robot interaction: a survey. *Foundations and Trends in Human-Computer Interaction*, 1(3), 203-275. - Seminal survey of human-robot interaction research.

4. Thrun, S. (2002). Probabilistic algorithms and the interactive museum tour-guide robot. *International Journal of Robotics Research*, 21(11), 911-923. - Early work on probabilistic robotics and real-world applications.

5. Pfeifer, R., & Bongard, J. (2006). *How the Body Shapes the Way We Think: A New View of Intelligence*. MIT Press. - Foundational work on embodied intelligence.

6. Brooks, R. A. (1991). Intelligence without representation. *Artificial Intelligence*, 47(1-3), 139-159. - Influential paper on behavior-based robotics.

7. Metta, G., Natale, L., Nori, F., Sandini, G., Vernon, D., Fadiga, L., ... & Oller, J. L. (2010). The iCub humanoid robot: An open-systems platform for research in cognitive development. *Neural Networks*, 23(8-9), 1125-1134. - Description of the iCub humanoid robot platform.

8. Deisenroth, M. P., Neumann, G., & Peters, J. (2013). A survey on policy search for robotics. *Foundations and Trends in Machine Learning*, 4(4), 287-342. - Comprehensive survey of policy search methods in robotics.

9. Szeliski, R. (2010). *Computer Vision: Algorithms and Applications*. Springer. - Comprehensive text on computer vision algorithms.

10. Slotine, J. J. E., & Li, W. (1991). *Applied Nonlinear Control*. Prentice Hall. - Classic text on nonlinear control theory.

## Conference Papers

1. Fox, D., Burgard, W., & Thrun, S. (1997). The dynamic window approach to collision avoidance. *IEEE Robotics & Automation Magazine*, 4(1), 23-33. - Seminal paper on collision avoidance for mobile robots.

2. Fox, D., Burgard, W., & Thrun, S. (1998). Active Markov localization for mobile robots. *Robotics and Autonomous Systems*, 25(3-4), 195-207. - Important work on robot localization.

3. Kajita, S., Kanehiro, F., Kaneko, K., Fujiwara, K., Harada, K., Yokoi, K., & Hirukawa, H. (2003). Biped walking pattern generation by using preview control of zero-moment point. *Proceedings 2003 IEEE/RSJ International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems*, 2, 1649-1655. - Key paper on bipedal walking control.

4. Rajpurkar, P., Zhang, J., Lopyrev, K., & Liang, P. (2016). SQuAD: 100,000+ questions for machine comprehension of text. *arXiv preprint arXiv:1606.05250*. - Important dataset for question answering systems.

5. He, K., Zhang, X., Ren, S., & Sun, J. (2016). Deep residual learning for image recognition. *Proceedings of the IEEE conference on computer vision and pattern recognition*, 770-778. - Seminal paper on deep residual networks.
